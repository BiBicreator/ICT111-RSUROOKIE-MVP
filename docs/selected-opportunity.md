# Selected Semester Project Opportunity

## Selected Project Title

RSU Campus Buddy (Smart Campus Chatbot for Student Services with Image Recognition)

## Target User

- New RSU students
- Current RSU students
- International students
- Campus visitors

## Problem Statement

Many students experience difficulty identifying campus buildings and finding accurate information about services, facilities, student clubs, operating hours, and university regulations. Information is often scattered across different websites, social media pages, and university offices, making it time-consuming and inconvenient to access.

## Current Alternative

Students currently rely on:
- Asking university staff or senior students
- Searching through university websites
- Using campus maps
- Looking for information on social media platforms

These methods can be slow, inconsistent, and inconvenient, especially for new students and visitors.

## Proposed IT Solution

RSU Campus Buddy is a smart campus information system that combines image recognition and chatbot technology. Users can upload a photo of an RSU building through a web application and instantly receive information about:

- Building name and location
- Available services and facilities
- Student clubs and organizations
- Operating hours
- Campus rules and regulations
- Frequently asked questions

The chatbot also allows users to ask campus-related questions and receive immediate responses.

## Why This Opportunity Was Selected

The team evaluated three project ideas using the NUF (New, Useful, Feasible) framework.

| Idea | New | Useful | Feasible | Total | Rank |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| RSU Campus Buddy | 8 | 9 | 8 | 25 | 1 |
| Student Second-Hand Marketplace | 6 | 8 | 9 | 23 | 2 |
| Student Roommate Matching & Housing Platform | 7 | 7 | 7 | 21 | 3 |

RSU Campus Buddy achieved the highest score because it solves a common problem experienced by students, provides a unique solution through image recognition and chatbot technology, and can be realistically developed within the semester using the team's skills and available resources.

## Why This Is Feasible Without IoT or Advanced Cybersecurity

The project can be developed as a web-based MVP using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and existing image recognition APIs. The system does not require physical sensors, IoT devices, or advanced cybersecurity infrastructure. Building information can be stored in a database and displayed through a chatbot interface, making the project achievable within the semester timeline.

## Possible MVP Type

**HTML/CSS/JavaScript Prototype**

The MVP will demonstrate:
- Building image upload
- Building identification
- Campus information display
- Chatbot interaction interface
