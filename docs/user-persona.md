# Lab 04 - User Persona

## 1. Persona Name

Aung Min, Year 2 ICT Student

## 2. User Type

Student who needs quick access to campus building information and navigation.

## 3. Background and Context

- Aung Min is a second-year ICT student at Rangsit University.
- He often visits different buildings for classes, club activities, and university services.
- Sometimes he cannot remember building names or locations, especially when classes are moved or he visits a building for the first time.
- He usually experiences this problem before class or when looking for university facilities.
- He frequently uses a smartphone, Google Maps, the university website, and messaging applications such as LINE.

## 4. Goals

- Identify an unknown campus building by taking or uploading a photo.
- Quickly find building information without asking other students.
- View available facilities, student clubs, opening hours, and floor maps.
- Find directions between campus buildings easily.

## 5. Pain Points from Lab 03 Evidence

| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Difficult to identify campus buildings | R03 | Students cannot recognize unfamiliar buildings on campus. |
| Campus information is scattered | R05 | Information is spread across multiple websites and social media pages. |
| Hard to find campus facilities | R07 | Students spend time searching for classrooms, offices, or service centers. |
| New students easily get lost | R09 | Freshmen are unfamiliar with campus locations and building names. |

## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds

| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Ask friends or seniors | Information may not always be available or accurate. | R03 |
| Search the university website | Requires multiple pages and takes time. | R05 |
| Use Google Maps | Does not provide detailed campus facilities or student club information. | R07 |
| Walk around campus | Time-consuming and inconvenient, especially before class. | R09 |

## 7. Design Implications

- The system should allow users to upload a building image for instant AI recognition.
- The chatbot should provide building names, facilities, opening hours, student clubs, and floor maps in one place.
- Navigation between buildings should be simple and easy to understand.
- The interface should be mobile-friendly because most students use smartphones.
- The MVP should focus on fast building recognition and accurate campus information.
- The system should avoid collecting unnecessary personal information from users.
